Cleaning the data and obtaining a Sample from the Data Set:

First we filter the data using trip distance as it is a very good indicator of possible mistakes or any extreme outliers that are not representative of the general populace. The data will be limited to New York State (NYS). NYS is 330 miles long and 285 miles wide at its longest and widest points respectively. Therefore, the minimum trip distance will be set to 0.50 miles and maximum at 300 miles, as a trip of over 300 miles is most certainly outside of NYS. We will also omit all NA values and all negative values.

Second we calculate a sample size. Using online sample size calculators such as SurveyMonkey we are able to obtain a sample size which is representative of our population for easier data manipulation. The Margin of Error is set at 1%, which is the range which your population may deviate from the sample, and Confidence Level at 95%, which is the probability that your sample accurately reflects your population. Most of these sample sizes come between 7900 and 9600, So we will use 10000 as the sample size for a cleaner data manipulation.

https://www.surveymonkey.com/mp/sample-size-calculator/
